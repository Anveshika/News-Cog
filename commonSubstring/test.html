The Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) surged to a massive win in the Delhi assembly polls on Tuesday, setting up a second stint for Arvind Kejriwal as chief minister and leaving the BJP licking its wounds in the city from which it rules the country.

The AAP was leading in 64 seats of the 70-member House, surpassing its own wildest expectations and beyond even the most optimistic exit polls, while the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) slumped to a distant second place with leads in just five seats.
